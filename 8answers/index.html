Overview

8Answers is a collaborative real estate management platform that helps developers, investors, project managers, and sales agents manage the complete life-cycle of a real estate project; from land acquisition and development planning to financial tracking, commission management, and property sales. The platform centralizes project operations, reporting, and marketplace functionality within a secure role-based ecosystem.

UI & Logo Exploration

Info

The 8Answers identity was designed to represent continuous solutions for complex real estate workflows. The logo symbol is derived from the number 8, shaped into an infinity-inspired form representing growth, collaboration, and connected workflows across projects, finances, stakeholders, and property sales.

The UI direction focused on clean dashboards, structured layouts, and simplified workflows for both technical and non-technical users. The primary color #0C8CE9 was chosen to represent trust, reliability, professionalism, and a sense of safety within financial and project management experiences.

Reflection

What Worked Well

  • Focused on creating a simple and intuitive user experience for stakeholders with limited software familiarity.
  • Designed clear dashboards and structured workflows that reduced complexity and improved visibility into project data.
  • Used familiar patterns and terminology to minimize the learning curve for users transitioning from tools like Tally and Zoho.
  • Role-based interfaces helped users focus only on information relevant to their responsibilities.
  • Desktop-first layouts allowed better presentation of financial data, reports, project layouts, and inventory management.

Challenges & Learnings

  • The initial mobile-first approach was difficult for users to navigate due to the large number of features and workflows compressed into a small screen.
  • Many target users were 40+ years old and preferred larger screens for reviewing financial information, layouts, and reports.
  • Complex project management workflows became harder to understand on mobile devices because of limited screen space and multiple navigation layers.
  • Through user testing and feedback, it became clear that a desktop experience aligned better with existing user behavior and work processes.

Key Outcome

  • Shifting from a mobile-focused design to a desktop-first experience was the biggest product and UX improvement.
  • The transition significantly improved usability, reduced confusion, simplified navigation, and increased adoption among project owners, partners, and managers.
  • The final design prioritized clarity over feature density, making the platform easier to learn and use for non-technical users.

Final Takeaway

Summary

This project taught me that successful product design is not about fitting more features into an interface, but about understanding user behavior and creating workflows that feel familiar, intuitive, and efficient. The decision to move from a mobile-first approach to a desktop-first experience reinforced the importance of validating design decisions through real user feedback rather than assumptions.
